# Trust And Reviews

This guide is for riders and drivers, not developers.

It explains how people build trust on Glitch a Hitch, how score works, and how reviews and reports fit into that picture.

## What Makes Someone Look Trustworthy

People usually look at a few things together:

- their score
- their driver rating
- their passenger rating
- how many rides they completed
- whether they communicate clearly

No single number tells the whole story.

## What Increases Score

Right now, score goes up for these things:

### 1. A Match Gets Confirmed

When the ride owner confirms a match:

- the driver gets **+100**
- the rider gets **+100**

This rewards both sides for turning a possible ride into a real plan.

### 2. You Receive a 5-Star Rating

When the other person gives you a 5-star rating after a completed ride:

- you get **+100**

This applies whether you were:

- the driver
- the rider

## What Does Not Increase Score

These things do **not** currently add score by themselves:

- posting a ride
- asking to join a ride
- completing a ride
- receiving 1–4 stars
- cancelling a ride
- reporting someone

## Reviews And Ratings

After a completed ride, people can leave:

- a star rating from 1 to 5
- an optional written comment

Your profile separates ratings into:

- **As a driver**
- **As a passenger**

That matters because somebody can be great in one role and weaker in the other.

## Reports

If something feels wrong, a ride can be reported for reasons such as:

- scam or fraud
- fake ride listing
- unsafe behavior
- harassment
- no-show
- spam
- misleading details
- payment issues
- other serious concerns

Reports are for moderation review.

Filing a report does **not** automatically lower another person’s score.

## Why Your Score Page Might Not Explain Everything

The score history page can explain score bumps when the reason is known and tracked.

Older score changes may not always be itemized if they happened before event tracking was added.

That is why you may see:

- tracked score events
- and a remaining balance that is not fully itemized yet
